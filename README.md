XPS17 Project Afterlife - 97XX

XPS17 Afterlife is a community-driven preservation and reverse-engineering project focused on the Dell XPS 17 97XX platform, beginning with the Dell XPS 17 9710/20/30.

The goal of this project is to document, archive, and recreate critical hardware and software resources that may disappear over time as official support fades. This includes PCB dimensions, connector pinouts, firmware behavior, thermal system analysis, board-level documentation, display information, driver archives, and replacement hardware development.

The project aims to preserve one of Dell’s most iconic “Cinema Display” laptop generations and keep these machines repairable, maintainable, and upgradeable long into the future.

Project Goals
- Archive drivers, firmware, BIOS versions, and utilities
- Document motherboard layouts and PCB dimensions
- Reverse engineer connectors and subsystem interfaces
- Preserve display panel information, EDIDs, and cable pinouts
- Analyze thermal and power delivery systems
- Develop aftermarket replacement PCBs and components
- Create open community documentation for repairs and modifications
- Support long-term preservation of the XPS 97XX ecosystem

Philosophy
Modern premium laptops are increasingly difficult to repair and preserve once manufacturers discontinue support. XPS17 Afterlife exists to ensure that technical knowledge surrounding the XPS 17 platform remains accessible to enthusiasts, repair technicians, engineers, and future hardware archivists.

Preserve. Document. Rebuild.
